3 Airbnb Insights that will make you smarter on your next Seattle or Boston booking

Imagine you have an upcoming holiday and you decide to travel! The first thing you need is a room and not everyone has a friend everywhere. Airbnb is a ubiquitous option these days. However, for the not so advanced user, Airbnb can be daunting and the very first thing you notice in Airbnb is the number of options you have from 1 bedroom apartments to villas, from downtown location to something more peaceful, the varied ranges in prices and ratings.

There are a lot of questions that come to your mind — Which room should you choose? What should be your budget? Do the ratings matter on Airbnb? And a whole lot of other questions. In this post, you will get 3 important insights which will help you become more intelligent, smarter, and break the myths when you decide to take a travel to the fabulous cities of Seattle or Boston and need an Airbnb room.

Before we set out, just a few acknowledgements. The dataset we used is as of February 09, 2019 and can be found here. It is provided to us by InsideAirbnb team. This work is done as a part of Udacity’s Data Scientist Nano Degree program which I highly recommend.

Question 1: Price vs Review Scores

Does price play a role in determining Review score ratings?

Short answer is yes, it does play a role. And, the answer remains the same across both cities — Seattle and Boston. The below plot gives us a clear indication of that.

We can see a couple of key things from the above plot. Firstly, as the price increases, the overall number of listings decrease. Secondly,

as the prices go up, the review ratings also remain high.

This implies that at higher prices, listings generally are able to satisfy the higher demands and expectations of the travelers staying in the rooms.

Question 2: How are houses described?

How do Seattle and Boston homeowners describe their houses?
Is there a difference in the way top reviewed houses are described?

Firstly, let’s check out Seattle listings. We’ll compare the overall words used in the summary of listings in Seattle vs the words used amongst the top 25 percentile of the listings based on their review ratings.

Amongst overall listings within Seattle, words at a higher frequency include:

beautiful, comfortable, private home, being near downtown, things being available at walking distance. Capitol Hill area also seems to be very popular.

Amongst the top 25 percentile homes in Seattle, majority of the words are similar. However, there are few interesting words which had higher frequency amongst those with high ratings. These are

Neighborhood, kitchen, large, and downtown.

Next, let’s dive into Boston listings. We’ll compare the overall words used in the summary of listings in Boston vs the words used amongst the top 25 percentile of the listings based on their review ratings.

Amongst overall listings within Boston, the summaries seem to have the following words at a higher frequency:

downtown, Back bay location, beautiful, park, South end, private home, and park.

Amongst the top 25 percentile homes in Boston, like Seattle, majority of the words are similar. However, here too there are few interesting words which had higher frequency. These are

Neighborhood, located, being near restaurant, and close.

We saved the best and most interesting question to the last.

Question 3: What are the best Airbnb neighborhoods to stay in Seattle and Boston?

We will use review rating scores to determine the best neighborhoods, and we’ll consider only those neighborhoods which have at least 10 listings as we’ll need some flexibility and choice in terms of bedrooms, price, etc.

As usual, let’s dive into Seattle first.

In Seattle, Southeast Magnolia clearly dominates with a significantly high number of listings than any other neighborhood with the 2nd best review rating scores.

Next up, let’s put Boston under the same lens.

Unlike Seattle, in Boston, there is no clear one neighborhood but rather few choices depending on what one wants. The balanced choices are between Jamaica Plain and South Boston Waterfront which have relatively high number of listings and high review ratings. However if one wants to go purely by highest review rating scores, then he/she has to look at Roslindale and West Roxbury neighborhoods.

I do hope that these insights give you much needed guidance when it’s time for you to head out and explore these two beautiful cities — Seattle and Boston.
